The Obsidian field guide
Turn today's AI reading into a second brain — in 10 minutes a day

Reading AI news is easy. Keeping it is the hard part. Here's the loop that turns a daily scroll into compounding knowledge — the same system behind Frontier.

The loop

Set up the vault (once)

Make three folders. That's the whole structure — topics live in tags, not folders.

my-brain/
├── raw/      ← inbox: paste anything, messy is fine
├── wiki/     ← distilled notes, the real brain
└── _meta/    ← your tag list + templates

Why so few folders? Because a note like "how OpenAI grows without ads" is at once marketing, strategy, and a case study. Folders force one home; tags let it live in all three.

The daily 10 minutes

1

Read Frontier, pick one or two

Don't try to keep everything. Choose the 1–2 things that actually change how you think.

2

Capture into raw/

Paste the link + one line on why it matters to you. Don't polish. Use this template:

---
source: <url>
tags: [ai-marketing]
---
# <one-line title>
Why it matters: <your take in a sentence>
Key point: <the one idea worth keeping>
3

Distill into wiki/

When a topic shows up two or three times, promote it: open a clean note in wiki/, write the idea in your own words, and [[link]] it to related notes. This is where understanding compounds.

4

Review weekly, connect the dots

Once a week, skim what you captured, merge duplicates, and add links. Obsidian's graph view will start showing clusters — that's your expertise taking shape.

Tag like a pro

Pick a small, fixed set of domain tags and stick to them. Mine:

#ai-marketing#ai-workflow#general-ai#career#investing

Rule of thumb: ≤ 5 tags per note · lowercase-hyphenated · prefer broad over narrow. When a tag keeps recurring, it's becoming one of your lanes.

That's it

Read → capture → distill → review. Ten minutes a day, and in a month you'll have a brain that's actually yours — not 200 unread bookmarks.
